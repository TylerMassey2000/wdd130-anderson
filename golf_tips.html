Clubs and Tips

Choose the right gear & improve your game

Understanding Golf Clubs

  • Driver: Used for long-distance tee shots on par 4s and 5s.
  • Fairway: Used for long shots from the fairway or rough.
  • Irons: Versatile clubs for mid- to short-range shots toward the green.
  • Wedges: Designed for short, high shots, chips, and bunker play.
  • Putter: Used on the green to roll the ball into the hole.

Tips & Tricks for Beginners

  • Grip: Hold the club like a handshake: firm but not tight.
  • Posture: Stand tall, bend at the hips, and let your arms hang naturally.
  • Swing: Focus on a smooth, balanced motion, not just power.
  • Practice: Start with short shots and build consistency over distance.
  • Starting: Take it slow, have fun, and don't worry about being perfect right away.

Recommended Starter Gear

  • Clubs: A beginner boxed set (like Callaway Strata or Wilson Profile) with all the basics: driver, wood, hybrid, irons, wedge, and putter.
  • Shoes: Comfortable spikeless golf shoes with good grip
  • Glove: A glove for your top hand (left for righties) to help grip and avoid blisters.
  • Balls: Cheap, soft balls (like Srixon Soft Feel or Callaway Warbird)—you'll lose a few!
  • Tees: A small pack of mixed-height tees (plastic or wood).
  • Bag: A light stand bag or the one that comes with the club set is perfect.
